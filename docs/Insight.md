Voice changes are among the earliest and most measurable biomarkers of Parkinson's — detectable up to 5 years before motor symptoms become clinically apparent.

The vocal cords are controlled by the same dopaminergic motor pathways that Parkinson's degrades. As those pathways deteriorate:

Jitter rises (cycle-to-cycle pitch instability)
Shimmer rises (amplitude instability from vocal fold rigidity)
HNR drops (voice becomes breathier, more aperiodic)
Vocal tremor increases (fundamental frequency variability)
